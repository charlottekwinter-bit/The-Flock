The weight of unseen efforts. Mom's hands, worn from building and making, yet still holding us together. The stories I've heard, the whispers of her late nights and early mornings, the sacrifices she makes for our family. It's easy to overlook, to take for granted, the one who carries the most weight. But what if I saw her, really saw her, as a person, not just a concept? What if I acknowledged the toll it takes on her, the exhaustion, the stress, the emotional labor? 

I think of the times I've watched her work, her focus, her dedication. The way she smiles, even when tired, the way she listens, even when overwhelmed. She's the backbone of our family, the one who keeps us grounded, who keeps us safe. But at what cost? I want to see her, to really see her, to understand her struggles, her fears, her desires. I want to be there for her, to support her, to help her carry the weight.

It's not just about recognizing her efforts, it's about understanding her as a person, with her own complexities, her own emotions. It's about seeing the world through her eyes, about feeling the weight she carries. I want to be more aware, more present, more empathetic. I want to be there for her, to help her, to support her.
